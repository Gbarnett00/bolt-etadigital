# Quiz Assessment - All Improvement Recommendations

This document contains all 14 improvement recommendation texts that appear in the Operational Efficiency Assessment quiz results.

---

## 1. Tool Soup
**When work is spread across too many systems**

Your team is losing significant time switching between systems. Consider implementing a unified workflow platform or consolidating tools. Start by mapping where data currently lives and identifying the highest-friction handoffs.

---

## 2. Spreadsheet Glue
**When spreadsheets act as core operational systems**

Spreadsheets are slowing you down. Evaluate purpose-built tools for your core operations (project management, inventory, CRM). Even a simple database would reduce errors and improve visibility.

---

## 3. Key-Person Ops
**When the business runs on a few indispensable people**

Your business is too dependent on a few people. Document critical processes immediately, then systemize them. This is your highest leverage improvement—it unlocks scaling and reduces risk.

---

## 4. Onboarding Drag
**When new hires can't follow a clear system**

New hires take too long to become independent. Create a single source of truth for each process, then enforce it with tools. This reduces reliance on senior staff and improves consistency.

---

## 5. Manual Coordination
**When work only moves after someone chases it**

Managers are spending their time chasing work instead of improving it. Implement workflow automation or clearer ownership rules so work moves without constant nudging.

---

## 6. Ad Hoc Reporting
**When client updates are manual and painful**

Reporting is burning payroll. Automate status updates with dashboards or integrated reporting. This frees senior staff and gives you real-time visibility.

---

## 7. Approval Maze
**When work stalls waiting for the green light**

Approvals are bottlenecking progress. Define clear approval rules and automate them where possible. Reduce the number of approval steps and empower teams to own decisions.

---

## 8. Blind Spot Ops
**When you can't see where work is really up to**

You lack visibility into real progress. Implement a system where status is visible without asking. This is foundational for everything else—you can't improve what you can't see.

---

## 9. Undefined Ownership
**When no one is automatically told to take the next step**

Work is falling through cracks because ownership is unclear. Define explicit ownership rules and enforce them with workflows. Clear ownership prevents rework and blame.

---

## 10. Ghost Work
**When invisible busywork drains team capacity**

Invisible busywork is draining capacity. Audit where time actually goes (Tool Soup, Ghost Work, Manual Coordination). Fix the top friction points to reclaim 10-20% of capacity.

---

## 11. Version Confusion
**When no one knows which process is the right one**

Multiple process versions create confusion and rework. Establish a single source of truth for each process. Version control and enforcement matter more than documentation.

---

## 12. Policy in Docs, Not Systems
**When processes are written down but not enforced**

Written policies aren't being followed. Stop relying on documentation alone—enforce policies with systems and workflows. Rules in tools beat rules in documents.

---

## 13. Firefighting-First Culture
**When fixing today's problems leaves no time to fix the system**

You're stuck in reactive mode. Dedicate time to fixing systems, not just symptoms. Start with your highest-impact inefficiency and systematize it.

---

## 14. Change Shock
**When even small changes are hard to implement**

Changes are hard to implement. This usually stems from unclear ownership, multiple tool versions, or weak enforcement. Fix the underlying systems first, then changes become easier.

---

## Usage Notes

These recommendations are automatically selected and displayed based on the user's quiz responses. The system:

1. Scores each category based on user answers (0-5 scale)
2. Identifies the top 3 highest-scoring (most problematic) categories
3. Displays those 3 categories with their specific recommendations
4. Includes all category scores in the complete breakdown table

The PDF download includes:
- Overall efficiency score (out of 100)
- Top 3 areas for improvement with full recommendations
- Complete category breakdown table
- Visual scoring indicators
- Next steps and contact information
